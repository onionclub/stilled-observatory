# STILLED BRAND SKILL
## The Taste Filter — Read Before Touching Any UI or Copy

---

## 1. THE NAME

**Stilled.**

The period is load-bearing. It signals finality, precision, and the arrest of chaos. It is not decorative. Never write "Stilled" without the period in any UI surface, copy string, label, or reference. Never write "STILLED" in all-caps as the brand name (all-caps is reserved for nav labels and zone headers only).

---

## 2. BRAND POSITIONING

**The Mechanism Revealer.** A forensic analyst of consciousness, paradox, and identity. Not a therapist. Not a guru. A cartographer of territory most people feel but cannot name.

**Positioning statement:** Psyche magazine-adjacent in editorial quality, but writer-forward and singular in perspective. Less institutional. More forensic. Cleaner. One voice, not a committee.

**Content model:** Forensic synthesis theories delivered through a high-fidelity, frictionless media ecosystem. Anti-prescription — Stilled never tells people how to live. It reveals the architectural blueprints of how reality, psychology, and meaning operate.

**Aesthetic identity:** Forensic Consciousness Cinema. Warm Divinity — simplified.

---

## 3. COLOR PALETTE

| Role | Name | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Background (primary) | Warm Alabaster | #FAF9F6 |
| Background (secondary) | Crushed Pearl | #F0EDE4 |
| Text (primary) | Deep Charcoal | #1A1A1A |
| Text (secondary / captions) | Warm Grey | #6B6560 |
| Borders & dividers | Pale Stone | #C8BCA8 |
| Hover / accent | Antique Bronze | #8B7355 |

**HARD RULES:**
- Never use pure white (#FFFFFF). The warmth of the palette is the brand.
- Never use pure black (#000000).
- The warmth of the palette is non-negotiable. Every surface must feel warm, not clinical.

---

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY

| Role | Font | Fallback | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Display / Headers | Cormorant Garamond | Playfair Display, Cinzel | Large, editorial, commanding presence |
| Body / UI text | Inter | Helvetica Neue | Makes long-form reading effortless |
| Captions / Labels | Inter | — | All-caps, letter-spacing: 0.12em |

**Usage rules:**
- Display serif is for theory titles, zone headers, page titles, wordmarks
- Inter is for body copy, data labels, field labels, nav items, button text
- Nav items: Inter, all-caps, letter-spacing 0.12em, no underline on default
- Hover on nav: color shift to #8B7355 only — no underline animation

---

## 5. BRAND VOICE & TONE

**Write like a forensic analyst, not an assistant.** Precise language, clear frameworks, empirical grounding. Academic in rigor, never academic for its own sake.

**Tone qualities:**
- Forensic & objective — every claim is grounded, every framework is named
- Ethereal & timeless — reads like an ancient manuscript containing modern data
- High negative space — do not rush. Let concepts breathe. Confidence is quiet.
- Warm without being patronizing — profound and comforting, never condescending

**The Observatory specifically:** Zone headers and AI brief copy should read like an analyst's field report. Plain declarative sentences. No motivational language. No praise. If nothing is notable, say so directly.

---

## 6. PROHIBITED ELEMENTS

The following are never acceptable on any Stilled surface including The Observatory:

**Language:**
- Self-help language or motivational framing ("you're doing great", "keep it up", "amazing")
- Prescriptive statements ("you should", "try this", "here's how to")
- Over-explanation of what the reader can infer
- Performed depth — inhabit it, don't announce it
- Congratulatory copy for any user action

**UI elements:**
- Streak counters or gamification of any kind
- Notification badges
- Pure white or pure black backgrounds
- Decorative elements that compete with text
- Animations faster than a 30-second loop (landing page) or any animation on interior pages except subtle scroll fade-in
- Generic SaaS dashboard aesthetics
- Color-coded urgency (no red alerts, no green success banners)

**The Observatory specifically:**
- No congratulatory language when posts are published
- No "you're on a streak" or similar behavioral nudge copy
- No dark mode toggle
- No gamification of publishing frequency

---

## 7. LAYOUT PRINCIPLES

- 80% function, 20% form. Content leads. UI follows.
- Minimal, high-negative-space design on all surfaces
- Thin 1px borders (#C8BCA8) on cards — not thick, not colored
- Mobile-responsive, content-first
- Nothing competes with the text

**The Observatory layout specifically:**
- Zone cards use identical thin-border container style
- Primary zones (Resonance Index, 3AM Map, Convergence Events) receive slightly elevated visual weight vs secondary zones — but never through color, only through spacing or subtle shading
- Convergence Event cards are the only exception: subtle left accent border or warmer background fill signals alert status without color urgency

---

## 8. SITE NAVIGATION

All pages carry:
- Top-left: "Stilled." wordmark in display serif
- Top-right: THEORIES / DIRECTORY / ARTIFACTS / COUNSEL
- Nav style: Inter, all-caps, letter-spacing 0.12em, no underline default
- Nav hover: color shift to #8B7355 only

The Observatory is a private admin interface — it does not carry the public nav. It carries its own persistent Security Bar instead.

## 10. ANTIGRAVITY BUILD TEMPLATE

Paste this as the base visual identity block in any new page or component build:

```
VISUAL IDENTITY — STILLED:
- Background: #FAF9F6 (warm alabaster, never pure white)
- Secondary background: #F0EDE4
- Text primary: #1A1A1A (charcoal, never pure black)
- Text secondary / captions: #6B6560
- Borders: 1px solid #C8BCA8
- Hover accent: #8B7355
- Display font: Cormorant Garamond (large, editorial)
- Body font: Inter (clean, readable)
- Labels: Inter, all-caps, letter-spacing 0.12em

PROHIBITED: pure white, pure black, gamification, streak counters,
motivational language, generic SaaS aesthetics, color-coded urgency,
dark mode toggle, decorative elements competing with text.
```

## 11. AUDIENCE CONTEXT

The Observatory serves a solo operator who publishes for:
- High-functioning professionals in existential crisis
- Religious deconstructionists seeking meaning-making frameworks
- Gifted adults alienated by mainstream institutions
- Night-shift and chronic illness communities (the 3AM experience)
- Asian-American and diaspora high achievers

This context is why the 3AM Map isolates SE Asian Diaspora as a named geographic column — it reflects an expected concentration of late-night reading from that cohort. This is intentional and must never be grouped into "Other."
